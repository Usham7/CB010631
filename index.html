Must Visit Places In Sri Lanka

Dambulla

Golden Cave Temple

Located in Sri Lanka's central region, is a historically significant tourist destination in the country, home to the UNESCO-listed Dambulla golden cave temple.

This amazing Buddhist monastery includes five natural granite caves and over 150 Buddha sculptures inside that exhibit the old workmanship in its purity, with lofty ceilings and stone walls that have been adorned with ancient holy objects and carvings.

Dambulla Golden Cave Temple, which has a history dating back to the first century BC, is one of the world's largest cave temples. Only three kilometers distant lies Ibbankatuwa, a prehistoric burial place with old civilization proof.

Dambulla is a city located 128 kilometers from Colombo International Airport.

Nuwara Eliya

Horton plains national park - The world end

Horton Plains, located in the country's central highlands, is a wonderful tourism destination in Sri Lanka, attracting visitors with a picturesque national park perched more than 2,200 meters above sea level.

In 1988, the plateau, which is rich in biodiversity and home to rare species of flora and wildlife, was designated as a tourist-friendly national park.

Horton is notable for being the headwaters of three main rivers in Sri Lanka, as well as the existence of a diverse range of rare flora and birds found only in this high-altitude location. Horton Plains, also known as 'Maha Eliya,' was named for one of the colonial governing classes and is located near a number of tourist attractions in the area, including Ohiya and Maha Eliya.

Kandy

The home of the sacred tooth relic

Kandy is both a cultural and regional arena of Sri Lanka, with the primary tourist attraction being the hallowed Buddhist temple, also known as the Tooth Relic Temple, which still retains the left canine tooth of Lord Buddha.

The city is used to seeing large groups of religious monks and followers wandering the temple. Kandy, also known as Maha Nuwara, is the capital of the central province and has a geographically stunning scenery surrounded by mountain peaks.

Kandy is 104 kilometers from Colombo International Airport and takes 3 hours to get by automobile. Ancient temples, cultural attractions, and animal reserves may all be found here.

NUWARA ELIYA

The tourist industry in Sri Lanka refers to Nuwara Eliya as "Little England".

The History Of Nuwara Eliya

This chilly hill area of Sri Lanka was formerly a wilderness of forest and rainforest, nestled in the shadow of the 2524-meter-high peak Pidurutalagala, also known as Mount Pedro. In 1819, British colonial commander John Davy "found" the hill region and intended to create a sanatorium there.

Samuel Baker, an adventurer who also discovered Lake Albert in Uganda and investigated the Nile River, built Nuwara Eliya in 1846.

Nuwara Eliya, which translates to "City of Light," quickly became a popular cool-climate getaway for the British, as well as a "home away from home" where they could produce English vegetables and fruits like lettuce and strawberries. They began growing coffee here as well, but when the coffee plants succumbed to a disease, they turned to tea

Nuwara Eliya Today

With its red telephone booths, pink brick Victorian post office, well-kept golf course, horse racing circuit, colonial houses, and rose gardens, Nuwara Eliya retains its English country village atmosphere.

Water buffalos graze the hillsides, green tea plants grow everywhere, chaotic traffic clogs the streets, the fragrance of rice and curry fills the air, and lively marketplaces and little stores line the streets.

    • Opening hours: 08 - 18
    • Cost: 200 Sri Lankan Rupees = 1,5 USD

    5. Take a stroll through Nuwara Eliya's downtown to get a sense of the English town atmosphere. There are various colonial-style residences, such as the old pink post office, where you may mail postcards to friends and family.
