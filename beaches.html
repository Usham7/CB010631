BEACHES

Mirissa Beach

Mirissa Beach is a sight to behold, gently curving around the bay in which it is situated, with lovely palm trees clustered along its golden beaches adding to the picturesque aspect. Its crystal blue waters are wonderful for swimming, and the little coral reef off the coast makes it a good snorkeling destination. In addition, at one end of the beach, some spectacular surfing may be found.

Despite the pristine atmosphere, there are several seashore pubs, restaurants, and hotels to choose from, all of which are nestled amid the trees. While the atmosphere is laid-back and pleasant, there are a few vibrant bars nearby that provide spectacular views of the sun sinking over the ocean.

Mirissa Beach, on Sri Lanka's south coast, offers wonderful dolphin and whale-watching cruises; viewing them in their natural habitat is an unforgettable experience. Mirissa Beach is one of Sri Lanka's highlights, with so much to see and do and such beautiful surroundings.

Unawatuna Beach

Unawatuna Beach, located just a short distance from Galle's city center, is one of the country's top beaches, with the dazzling Indian Ocean bordering its gorgeous golden sands.The little community that stands alongside the beach has a very relaxed and pleasant attitude. There are a variety of taverns, restaurants, and motels to suit all budgets.

It has a lot going for it, including some wonderful dive locations where you can do scuba diving and snorkeling. It is one of the most popular beaches in Sri Lanka among residents and visitors alike. You could even see sea turtles breeding along the shore if you arrive during the right season.

On top of that, if you can pull yourself away from Unawatuna's gorgeous beach, you can always visit Galle's remarkable historical monuments and cultural attractions.

Arugam Bay Beach

If you enjoy surfing, there is no better site in Sri Lanka to visit than Arugam Bay. The gorgeous palm tree-lined beaches that span throughout the majority of the surrounding coastline are constantly crashing with large waves.

Despite the fact that Arugam Bay is becoming increasingly famous as a tourist destination, it remains charmingly unspoilt, with few major hotels or resorts. This is part of what makes it so enticing; the little caf√©s, restaurants, and cabanas retain their local flavor.

There are several activities to select from in addition to enjoying the lovely beaches. Arugam Bay offers it all, whether it's a tour of the gorgeous Pottuvil Lagoon, a sunset at Elephant Rock, or a safari in Yala National Park where you may see real elephants.
